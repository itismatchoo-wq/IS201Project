TRIATHLONS

A Brief Overview

A triathlon is a race that combines swimming, biking, and running back-to-back. The distances can range from short, beginner-friendly sprints to the massive Ironman events, but the idea is the same. Part of the appeal is the variety. Training never feels stale because you’re constantly switching and working on trhee disciplines. One day you’re in the pool, the next you’re out on the bike, and later you’re working on your run.

Triathlons have also become more approachable than people think. Most cities offer local races designed for first-timers, and there’s a strong sense of community from clubs and training groups that help people ease into the sport. Beyond the fitness side, triathlons give people a clear, motivating challenge. Setting a race date adds purpose to your workouts, and crossing the finish line is a rush that keeps many athletes hooked.

At the end of the day, triathlons are popular because they’re tough, rewarding, and surprisingly welcoming—an ideal mix for anyone looking to push themselves in a new way.

The Gear

Triathlons also happen to require a lot more equipment to participate due to the three events all mixed into one. Below is a list of gear commonly used for each event.

  1. Swim
    • Swimsuit or tri-suit
    • Goggles
    • Swim cap
    • Wetsuit (for open water swims)
  2. Bike
    • Road bike or triathlon bike
    • Helmet
    • Cycling shoes
    • Water bottle
    • Repair kit (spare tube, tire levers, pump)
  3. Run
    • Running shoes
    • Running clothes (shorts, shirt)
    • Hat or visor
    • Sunglasses
  4. Fueling
    • Energy gels or bars
    • Electrolyte drinks
    • Easily digestible fruits (mandarin oranges, bananas, etc.)
